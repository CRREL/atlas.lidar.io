ATLAS

A remote LiDAR system capturing 3D time-lapse data of the Helheim Glacier in southeast Greenland.

Status

This information is current as of {{atlasStatus.last_heartbeat_received | date:"medium" }}. All times on this page are in your browser's local time zone. Every hour, ATLAS sends a "heartbeat" message, via satellite connection, that contains information on current power levels, environmental conditions, and more. The remote cameras use their own satellite connections to transmit images back to host servers.

Power

ATLAS's power system consists of six large solar panels, providing about 2kWp, and two EFOY fuel cell generators, providing about 160W. During the summer months, the solar supply provides more than enough power for the system. As the sun descends during the shoulder seasons, the EFOY fuel cells take over and provide power into the winter.
